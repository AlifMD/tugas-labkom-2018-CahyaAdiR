What is the dangers of ACETONE?

Acetone is a clear, colorless liquid that is an extremely flammable chemical. Acetone is a common item, but it can make some serious risks. When a person uses acetone and inhale the acetone smell, it will indirectly cause lungs irritation. For short-term, if often inhaled can make headache, irritation of the eyes(characterized by burning, redness, tearing, inflammation, and possible corneal injury), and nose. For the long-term can damage the nervous in the nose, throat, and brain. The long term can make our skin dry, cracks and also damage the nerveous system. Explosion can happen when acetone is mixed with hydrogen peroxide, nitric acid and sulfur dichloride.To avoid occupational safety hazards, we must know what chemicals to segregate from acetone. To throw away the liquid acetone, you need to take the acetone to a hazardous waste treatment.

The storage of ACETONE :

Acetone Storage must be stored in a cool, dry, and well-ventilated chemical storage building. The chemical storage building must be located in an area where fire hazards are low. The handling and storage of Acetone is very important and must take caution. Keep away from heat, keep away from sources of fire, keep away from sun, use the correct personal protective equipment. If swallowed immediately medical contact. Avoid with eye contact and skin. Keep away from oxidizing reagents, reducing reagents, acids, and alkalis.
